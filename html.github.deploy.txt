    <section class="section">
      <h2>How This Work Was Produced</h2>
      <p class="section-intro">
        All of the above projects were developed through extended conversations inside ChatGPT, using the model as a
        mixed-role assistant: researcher, opponent, and editor. The workflow typically looked like this:
      </p>
      <div class="grid grid-2">
        <article class="card">
          <div class="card-header">
            <div class="card-title">1. Framing & Hypothesis</div>
            <span class="badge">Phase</span>
          </div>
          <div class="card-body">
            <ul>
              <li>Define the core strategic question in plain language.</li>
              <li>State an initial hypothesis or intuition to test.</li>
              <li>Set constraints: time horizon, geography, actors, and acceptable assumptions.</li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </article>

        <article class="card">
          <div class="card-header">
            <div class="card-title">2. Structured Decomposition</div>
            <span class="badge">Phase</span>
          </div>
          <div class="card-body">
            <ul>
              <li>Break the problem into sub-modules (finance, military, political, informational).</li>
              <li>Build taxonomies and matrices instead of linear essays.</li>
              <li>Cross-walk findings against historical analogues where possible.</li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </article>

        <article class="card">
          <div class="card-header">
            <div class="card-title">3. Red-Teaming & Stress-Testing</div>
            <span class="badge">Phase</span>
          </div>
          <div class="card-body">
            <ul>
              <li>Ask the model to argue the opposite case or identify failure modes.</li>
              <li>Introduce shocks (e.g., canal closures, commodity spikes, alliance breakdowns).</li>
              <li>Revise the framework until it survives plausible adversarial scenarios.</li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </article>

        <article class="card">
          <div class="card-header">
            <div class="card-title">4. Packaging & Teaching</div>
            <span class="badge">Phase</span>
          </div>
          <div class="card-body">
            <ul>
              <li>Convert raw notes into concise briefs, slides, or teaching units.</li>
              <li>Highlight decision-relevant insights rather than encyclopedic detail.</li>
              <li>Design prompts for students, policymakers, or analysts to engage the material.</li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </article>
